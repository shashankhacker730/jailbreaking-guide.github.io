Phoenix

This method only supports Apple A5 and A6 Devices on iOS 9.3.5 and 9.3.6. If you are on an older version of iOS 9 please use Home Depot instead.
For this guide you will need the following;
  • The Phoenix IPA
  • Cydia Impactor
  • A Free Developer Apple ID
Step 1: Impactor
  1. Connect your iOS device to your Mac or PC (Apple OEM cables are recommended)
  2. Start Cydia Impactor, then drag the Phoenix IPA onto the window
  3. You will be prompted for your Apple ID credentials (this one has to be enrolled in a beta program (free or paid)
  4. Once you've entered your credentials in correctly, Cydia Impactor should install Phoenix onto your iOS Device.
If this fails, try again with your correct Apple ID or a different charge/sync cable or go to the Troubleshooting section.
Step 2: Phoenix Jailbreak
  1. Once the Phoenix App has been installed onto your iOS Device, make sure to delete all OTA updates if any
  2. Open Settings then navigate to General > Profiles & Device Management. The Apple ID used to install the app should show.
  3. Tap on the Profile, then tap Verify (or Trust) and follow all prompts onscreen
  4. Now that Phoenix has been verified, open it on your Home Screen
  5. Once open, tap Jailbreak and follow all prompts onscreen
    6. If the Jailbreak fails on your device and you're on iOS 9.3.6, manually set the offsets for your device. Offsets for A5 and offsets for A6
Make sure to use Provided Offsets, do not set your own (unless you know what you're doing)
If this fails, after the respring hold the Sleep/Wake Button and the Home Button and force restart. After your device has rebooted, try again.
Step 3: Cydia
  1. After your device resprings, you should have Cydia installed on your Home Screen
  2. Launch Cydia and let it (re)load its packages. If you are prompted for an upgrade, tap Upgrade All

Once all upgrades are completed and you've resprung, you are free to use any (compatible) Cydia tweaks

Also feel free to check out the Jailbreaking Essentials
If Cydia or any apps installed from Cydia start crashing after a respring or reboot, open Phoenix and then tap Kickstart Jailbreak. Your device should be re-jailbroken after the respring.
