iOS 13 Beta Install

iOS 13 only supports iPhone 6S and newer, iPad Air 2 and newer, iPad 5 and newer, iPad Mini 4 and newer, iPod Touch 7, and all iPad Pros. If you are using an older device, you cannot run iOS 13.
There is no official iOS 13 Jailbreak. Updating to iOS 13 will remove your Jailbreak.
Before continuing this guide, please make sure you have backed up your iOS Device via iTunes and/or iCloud.
Make sure to keep your device charged while following this guide. iOS 13 may take a while to install.
Step 1: Preparing your Device
  1. Open Settings and navigate to Account Name > iCloud > Find my i(Phone/Pad/Pod Touch)
  2. Turn off Find My i(Phone/Pad/Pod Touch)
Find my iPhone will need to be disabled in order to restore/update your iOS Device.
Step 2: Preparing your Computer
  1. Firstly, you'll need to download the IPSW for your device. If you're not a registered developer, you'll need to find it somewhere. We can't link to it here, but we think google.com might help
  2. Once you've downloaded your IPSW, make sure it is at least 3GB or so
The next part of this guide will be specific to your operating system. Linux is not supported as far as we know.
If you are running macOS, you will need to have downloaded the Xcode 11 Beta or be running macOS Catalina. Click here to follow the macOS section of this guide.
For Windows users, click here for the 3uTools Guide (Recommended) or click here for the iDeviceRestore Guide.

Step 2A: Installing iOS 13 (macOS)

To install iOS 13 on your iOS Device with a Mac, you will need the Xcode 11 Beta or have macOS Catalina installed. You will also need to make sure iTunes is up to date.
Unfortunately, we do not know how to do this in macOS Catalina. It would help us greatly if any of your macOS Catalina users would contribute to the guide.
  1. Open iTunes
  2. Plug in your iOS Device with its Charge/Sync Lightning or USB-C cable
  3. Navigate to your iOS Device
  4. Hold the option key and click on Restore iPhone
  5. In the popup Finder window, navigate to and select your downloaded iOS 13 IPSW
    6. Do not disconnect your iOS Device until the installation has concluded.
If all goes well, you should have iOS 13 installed on your iOS Device.

Step 2B: Installing iOS 13 (Windows | 3uTools)

As always, when using third-party software, you accept any and all risks when using said software to flash your iOS Device.
  1. Download 3uTools
  2. Open 3uTools and connect your iOS Device with its Charge/Sync Lightning or USB-C Cable
  3. Navigate to the Flash & JB section
    4. If you've already downloaded your IPSW, you can selet Import and navigate to your IPSW. If not, select Download and download the IPSW for your iOS Device.
  4. Once the IPSW has been verified, you can begin to flash your iOS Device with the iOS 13 Beta
    5. Do NOT disconnect your iOS Device until 3uTools confirms that iOS 13 has been flashed
If all goes well, you should have iOS 13 installed on your iOS Device.

Step 2C: Installing iOS 13 (Windows | iDeviceRestore)

As always, when using third-party software, you accept any and all risks when using said software to flash your iOS Device.
  1. Download iDeviceRestore GUI
  2. Open iDeviceRestore GUI and connect your iOS Device with its Charge/Syc Lightning or USB-C Cable
  3. Select Open and navigate to and select your downloaded IPSW
    4. If you have not downloaded your IPSW, select Download IPSW and download the IPSW for your iOS Device.
    If you would like to erase all your data and install iOS 13, check the box Restore iPhone.
  4. Once all parameters have been set, click Run
    5. This process will take anywhere from 10 - 40 minutes. Do NOT disconnect your iOS Device during the installation.
After iOS 13 has been installed, your iOS Device will enter recovery mode. Let it recover your data and reboot. You should now be running iOS 13.
